# Review Generation Workflow — The Flooring Folks

## Overview

A simple, repeatable system to consistently generate 8–10 new Google reviews per month. This is one of the highest-impact things you can do for your local SEO and online reputation.

---

## The 4-Step Workflow

### Step 1: Job Complete → Send SMS (Within 2 Hours)

**When:** As soon as the job is wrapped up and the customer has signed off / paid
**What:** Send the SMS template (review-request-sms.md)
**Who:** Ryan sends it personally from his phone
**How:**
1. Finish the job, do a walkthrough with the customer
2. Make sure they're happy before you leave
3. Within 2 hours, send the SMS from your phone
4. Log the customer name + date in a simple spreadsheet or note

**If the job finishes in the evening:** Wait until the next morning between 9–10 AM to send.

---

### Step 2: No Review After 3 Days → Send Email

**When:** 3 days after the SMS was sent, if no review has been posted
**What:** Send the email template (review-request-email.md)
**How:**
1. Check your Google Business Profile for new reviews
2. If the customer hasn't reviewed, send the email
3. Personalize it with their name and service type
4. Include the QR code image in the email

---

### Step 3: No Review After 7 Days → Send Follow-Up

**When:** 7 days after the initial SMS (4 days after the email)
**What:** Send the follow-up template (review-followup.md) — SMS or email, your choice
**Rules:**
- **Only ONE follow-up.** If they don't review after this, let it go.
- Skip the follow-up if the customer had any issues or complaints
- Keep it light and friendly — never pushy

---

### Step 4: Review Received → Respond Within 24 Hours

**When:** As soon as you see a new review on Google
**What:** Use the appropriate response template from review-response-templates.md
**How:**
1. Check your Google Business Profile daily (or set up notifications)
2. Match the star rating to the right template
3. Personalize with customer name and service type
4. Post your response

**Response time matters.** Google rewards businesses that respond to reviews, and customers notice.

---

## Monthly Goal: 8–10 New Reviews

### How to Hit This Target

- **If you do ~15–20 jobs per month**, and ~50% of customers leave a review after the SMS alone, you'll hit 8–10 easily
- **The 3-step sequence** (SMS → email → follow-up) typically converts 40–60% of customers
- **Track it weekly** — if you're falling behind, be more consistent with Steps 2 and 3

### Quick Math
| Jobs/Month | SMS Response Rate | Email Boost | Follow-Up Boost | Total Reviews |
|------------|-------------------|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| 15 | 6 (40%) | +2 | +1 | ~9 |
| 20 | 8 (40%) | +3 | +1 | ~12 |

## How to Create Your Direct Google Review Link

This is the link that takes customers straight to the review form — no searching, no friction.

### Method 1: Using Google Maps (Easiest)

1. **Search for your business** on Google Maps (search "The Flooring Folks Florence AL")
2. **Click on your business listing** to open it
3. **Click "Write a review"** (the star icon)
4. **Copy the URL** from your browser's address bar
5. That's your review link! It will look something like:
   `https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=ChIJ...`

### Method 2: Using Your Place ID (More Reliable)

1. **Go to:** [https://developers.google.com/maps/documentation/places/web-service/place-id](https://developers.google.com/maps/documentation/places/web-service/place-id)
2. **Search for "The Flooring Folks"** in Florence, AL
3. **Copy your Place ID** (it looks like: `ChIJN1t_tDeuEmsRUsoyG83frY4`)
4. **Build your link:**
   ```
   https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID_HERE
   ```
5. **Test it** — open the link in a private/incognito browser to make sure it works

### Method 3: From Google Business Profile

1. **Log in** to [Google Business Profile](https://business.google.com)
2. **Click "Ask for reviews"** (on the home screen or under "Customers" → "Reviews")
3. **Copy the short link** Google generates for you
4. This is the easiest option — Google gives you a ready-made shareable link

**Save this link somewhere safe — you'll use it in every review request.**

## How to Create a QR Code for Your Review Link

### Step-by-Step

1. **Copy your Google review link** (from above)
2. **Go to one of these free QR code generators:**
   - [https://www.qr-code-generator.com](https://www.qr-code-generator.com)
   - [https://goqr.me](https://goqr.me)
   - [https://www.canva.com/qr-code-generator](https://www.canva.com/qr-code-generator)
3. **Paste your review link** into the URL field
4. **Customize** (optional): Add your logo, change colors to match your brand
5. **Download as PNG** (high resolution, at least 300x300 pixels)
6. **Test it** — scan with your phone camera before using

### Where to Use the QR Code

- **In review request emails** (below the text link)
- **On a printed thank-you card** to leave with customers after a job
- **On invoices or receipts**
- **On your website's review page**
- **On a small sign in your truck/vehicle**

### QR Code Pro Tips

- **Keep it simple** — don't overload it with logos or colors; simpler QR codes scan faster
- **Make it at least 1 inch x 1 inch** when printed
- **Always test before printing** — QR codes that don't work are worse than no QR code
- **Use a URL shortener** (like bit.ly) before generating the QR code if your review link is very long
